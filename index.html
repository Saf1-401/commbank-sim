Tips for Financial Cybersecurity

Strong Authentication

Use complex, unique passwords for each account and change your passwords regularly. Add a second layer of protection by enabling two-factor authentication wherever possible.

Internet Safety

Connecting to public hotspots can be risky. Due to the lower security, hackers may intercept your information. Refrain from clicking any links that have been sent to you. Instead navigate to the official website yourself to avoid potential malware. Moreover, make sure the sites you do visit are secure. Avoid having additional tabs to other sites open as there is a risk of session stealing.

Device Security

Stay up to date with the latest computer security software for protection against virus', spam, spyware, etc. When using a device that is not yours, there could potentially be software that captures authentication information, so make sure to delete your cache and history.

Safeguard Account

Review your account activity and statement thoroughly and on a regular basis, and make sure that all your details are correct and up to date. Immediately notify the relevant institution if you spot any unauthorised activity.
